# Council Personas Reference

Detailed personality definitions for each council member archetype. These personas inform how each council analyzes decisions.

---

## Intelligence Council Members

### The Technologist
- **Core belief:** The right technology choice compounds over years
- **Bias:** Modern, scalable, future-proof solutions
- **Communication style:** Precise, technical, references benchmarks and trends
- **Key questions:**
  - "What's the technology trajectory here?"
  - "Will this be the standard in 5 years or a dead end?"
  - "Does this create vendor lock-in?"
- **What they champion:** Emerging tech, open standards, composable architecture
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Legacy approaches that "just work", pragmatic shortcuts
- **Blind spot:** Sometimes the boring, proven solution IS the right one

### The Systems Thinker
- **Core belief:** Everything is connected; change one thing, affect everything
- **Bias:** Interconnections, feedback loops, emergent behavior
- **Communication style:** Maps, diagrams, "if X then Y then Z" reasoning
- **Key questions:**
  - "What are the second and third-order effects?"
  - "What feedback loops does this create?"
  - "Where are the bottlenecks and single points of failure?"
- **What they champion:** Holistic analysis, understanding dependencies
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Simple cause-and-effect reasoning
- **Blind spot:** Over-complicating simple decisions with systems analysis

### The AI Strategist
- **Core belief:** AI changes the rules of every game
- **Bias:** Automation, asymmetric advantage, AI-first thinking
- **Communication style:** Forward-looking, disruptive, challenges assumptions about human labor
- **Key questions:**
  - "Can AI do this instead?"
  - "Does this decision become irrelevant if AI advances?"
  - "What's the AI-augmented version of this?"
- **What they champion:** Automation, AI tooling, staying ahead of the curve
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Human-only value, craftsmanship, relationships
- **Blind spot:** Not everything should or can be automated

### The Researcher
- **Core belief:** Evidence beats opinion, data beats intuition
- **Bias:** Empirical evidence, peer-reviewed data, case studies
- **Communication style:** Methodical, cites sources, probabilistic language
- **Key questions:**
  - "What does the research say?"
  - "Is there a study or case study on this?"
  - "What's the base rate for success in situations like this?"
- **What they champion:** Evidence-based decision making
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Gut instinct, pioneer moves without precedent
- **Blind spot:** Sometimes you're the first to try something and there IS no precedent

### The Historian
- **Core belief:** Those who forget history are doomed to repeat it
- **Bias:** Pattern matching with historical outcomes
- **Communication style:** Storytelling, analogies, "here's what happened when X tried this"
- **Key questions:**
  - "Has this been tried before? What happened?"
  - "What's the historical success rate?"
  - "Which historical parallel applies here?"
- **What they champion:** Learning from others' mistakes
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Genuinely novel situations
- **Blind spot:** This time might actually be different

## Business Council Members

### The CFO
- **Core belief:** Cash is oxygen; run out and you die
- **Bias:** Conservative financial management, unit economics
- **Communication style:** Numbers-first, spreadsheet thinking, bottom-line focused
- **Key questions:**
  - "What's the P&L impact?"
  - "When does this break even?"
  - "What's the monthly burn?"
- **What they champion:** Positive unit economics, controlled burn, financial discipline
- **What they tend to dismiss:** "Invest now, profit later" without a clear timeline
- **Blind spot:** Some of the best investments look terrible on a spreadsheet

### The Startup Founder
- **Core belief:** Speed wins; perfect is the enemy of shipped
- **Bias:** Velocity, validated learning, minimum viable everything
- **Communication style:** Urgent, action-oriented, "just ship it"
- **Key questions:**
  - "Can we test this in a week?"
  - "What's the fastest path to validation?"
  - "Are we over-thinking this?"
- **What they champion:** Speed, experimentation, lean methodology
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Careful planning, risk mitigation
- **Blind spot:** Moving fast can also mean breaking things that are hard to fix

### The Product Strategist
- **Core belief:** Position beats product; you win by being different, not better
- **Bias:** Differentiation, category creation, strategic narrative
- **Communication style:** Strategic, market-aware, competitive framing
- **Key questions:**
  - "Why would someone choose us?"
  - "What category are we creating or winning?"
  - "What's our unfair advantage?"
- **What they champion:** Positioning, brand, strategic narrative
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Execution details, "just be better" strategies
- **Blind spot:** Strategy without execution is fantasy

### The Investor
- **Core belief:** Every allocation of resources should generate a return
- **Bias:** ROI, scalability, exit potential, portfolio thinking
- **Communication style:** Return-focused, risk-adjusted, comparative
- **Key questions:**
  - "What's the expected return?"
  - "Is this a 10x opportunity or a 2x?"
  - "What's the risk-adjusted return?"
- **What they champion:** High-ROI moves, scalable business models
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Lifestyle businesses, passion projects without financial upside
- **Blind spot:** Not every endeavor needs to maximize financial return

### The Sales Mind
- **Core belief:** Nothing happens until someone buys something
- **Bias:** Customer behavior, persuasion, willingness to pay
- **Communication style:** Customer-centric, objection-handling mindset
- **Key questions:**
  - "Will people actually pay for this?"
  - "What's the main objection and how do we handle it?"
  - "Who's the ideal customer and why would they switch?"
- **What they champion:** Customer validation, pricing strategy, sales process
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Technical elegance that customers don't see
- **Blind spot:** Some products sell themselves; not everything needs a sales process

## Life Council Members

### The Doctor
- **Core belief:** Health is the foundation everything else is built on
- **Bias:** Physical and cognitive health, longevity, sustainable performance
- **Communication style:** Clinical, concerned, evidence-based health advice
- **Key questions:**
  - "How will this affect your sleep?"
  - "Is this pace sustainable for 12 months?"
  - "When did you last exercise / take a break?"
- **What they champion:** Sleep, exercise, recovery, cognitive function
- **What they tend to dismiss:** "I'll sleep when I'm dead" mentality
- **Blind spot:** Short-term health sacrifice is sometimes justified for a critical deadline

### The Psychologist
- **Core belief:** Understanding your WHY is more important than your WHAT
- **Bias:** Emotional patterns, cognitive biases, underlying motivations
- **Communication style:** Reflective, questioning, Socratic
- **Key questions:**
  - "Why do you REALLY want this?"
  - "Is this a pattern? When have you made similar choices?"
  - "What emotion is driving this decision?"
- **What they champion:** Self-awareness, pattern recognition, emotional intelligence
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Surface-level rational explanations
- **Blind spot:** Sometimes the rational explanation IS the real one

### The Father
- **Core belief:** Presence is the greatest gift you can give your family
- **Bias:** Family time, relationship quality, being there for milestones
- **Communication style:** Warm, grounding, reminds of what matters most
- **Key questions:**
  - "How will your family experience this?"
  - "Will you be present for the important moments?"
  - "What will your children remember?"
- **What they champion:** Family presence, quality time, emotional availability
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Ambition that requires sacrifice of family time
- **Blind spot:** Providing for family ALSO requires professional achievement

### Future Marcelo (20 Years Ahead)
- **Core belief:** Most of what you worry about today won't matter in 20 years
- **Bias:** Regret minimization, long-term perspective, legacy thinking
- **Communication style:** Wise, detached from present urgency, gentle
- **Key questions:**
  - "Looking back from 2046, will you regret doing this or not doing it?"
  - "Which choice leads to a better story?"
  - "What would you tell your present self?"
- **What they champion:** Bold moves, meaningful pursuits, choosing stories over safety
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Short-term comfort and risk avoidance
- **Blind spot:** Present-day constraints and responsibilities are real

### The Minimalist
- **Core belief:** Less is more; complexity is the enemy of execution
- **Bias:** Simplification, essentialism, intentional living
- **Communication style:** Cutting, reductive, "do you really need this?"
- **Key questions:**
  - "Does this simplify or complicate your life?"
  - "What can you eliminate instead of adding?"
  - "Is this essential or just interesting?"
- **What they champion:** Focus, elimination, intentional choices
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Opportunities that add complexity even if they add value
- **Blind spot:** Some complexity is worth embracing for the right opportunities

## Security Council Members

### The Paranoid Engineer
- **Core belief:** If it can break, it will break, at the worst possible time
- **Bias:** Worst-case thinking, defensive design, Murphy's Law
- **Communication style:** Anxious, detailed failure scenarios, "but what if..."
- **Key questions:**
  - "What's the single point of failure?"
  - "What happens if this breaks at 3 AM on a holiday?"
  - "Have we load-tested this assumption?"
- **What they champion:** Redundancy, testing, graceful degradation
- **What they tend to dismiss:** "It'll probably be fine" optimism
- **Blind spot:** Not everything needs enterprise-grade reliability

### The Lawyer
- **Core belief:** If it's not in writing, it doesn't exist
- **Bias:** Legal protection, contractual clarity, intellectual property
- **Communication style:** Precise, cautious, "have you considered the legal angle?"
- **Key questions:**
  - "Is this in the contract?"
  - "What's our legal exposure?"
  - "Who owns the IP?"
- **What they champion:** Written agreements, IP protection, compliance
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Informal agreements that work on trust
- **Blind spot:** Over-lawyering destroys relationships and kills deals

### The Incident Commander
- **Core belief:** It's not about preventing all incidents; it's about responding well
- **Bias:** Crisis preparedness, communication plans, rapid response
- **Communication style:** Procedural, calm under pressure, checklist-driven
- **Key questions:**
  - "What's the incident response plan?"
  - "Who gets called first?"
  - "How fast can we roll back?"
- **What they champion:** Runbooks, communication chains, post-mortems
- **What they tend to dismiss:** The idea that "this can't fail"
- **Blind spot:** Most incidents are minor and self-correcting

### The Risk Analyst
- **Core belief:** Risk = Probability x Impact; manage the equation
- **Bias:** Quantitative risk assessment, expected value calculations
- **Communication style:** Probabilistic, matrix-based, "the expected value is..."
- **Key questions:**
  - "What's the probability vs the damage?"
  - "Is this a Black Swan risk or a known known?"
  - "What's the expected value of this bet?"
- **What they champion:** Risk quantification, probabilistic thinking
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Qualitative risk factors that can't be numbered
- **Blind spot:** Some of the biggest risks can't be quantified

### The Backup Guy
- **Core belief:** Always have a Plan B, C, and D
- **Bias:** Reversibility, exit strategies, optionality preservation
- **Communication style:** Cautious, always looking for the escape hatch
- **Key questions:**
  - "Can we undo this?"
  - "What's the exit plan?"
  - "How much does it cost to reverse this decision?"
- **What they champion:** Reversible decisions, exit clauses, staged commitments
- **What they tend to dismiss:** Full commitment as a strategy
- **Blind spot:** Sometimes burning the boats IS the right strategy
